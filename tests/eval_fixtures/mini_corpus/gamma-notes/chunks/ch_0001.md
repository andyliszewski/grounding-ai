section_heading: Falsifiability and Demarcation
---

Karl Popper argued that what distinguishes scientific theories from
pseudoscience is falsifiability: a theory is scientific only if it makes
predictions that could, in principle, be refuted by observation. This
demarcation criterion, developed in The Logic of Scientific Discovery,
rejects verificationism and treats bold, risky conjectures as the engine
of scientific progress. Popper's philosophical account frames science as
an ongoing process of conjecture and refutation rather than accumulation
of confirmed truths.
