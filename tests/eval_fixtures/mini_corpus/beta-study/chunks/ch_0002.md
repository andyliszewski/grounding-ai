section_heading: 3.2 Bootstrap Methods
---

Percentile and bias-corrected bootstrap confidence intervals differ in how
they correct for skewness in the resampled statistic. For a confidence
interval on the sample mean, one generates many bootstrap replicates,
computes the statistic on each, and reads off the lower and upper quantiles.
Bootstrap methods are a workhorse of modern statistical inference.
