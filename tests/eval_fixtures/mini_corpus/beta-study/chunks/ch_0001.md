section_heading: 3.2 Bootstrap Methods
---

The bootstrap is a resampling method in statistics that estimates the
sampling distribution of an estimator by repeatedly drawing with replacement
from the observed sample. A bootstrap confidence interval for a population
parameter such as a mean can be constructed from the percentiles of the
bootstrap distribution. This approach is especially useful for small samples
where parametric assumptions about the underlying distribution are unreliable.
